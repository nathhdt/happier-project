Background - Why Happier?

In general, mental disorders have increased by four times since the coronavirus pandemic. Therefore, there is a need for a large part of the population to get help following these events.

More than 5000 scientific papers correspond to research on the psychological impact of the epidemic and containment. Happier offers a solution to diagnose states of depression or anxiety with a free application that anyone can access.

We offer tools to help you better manage your stress, organize your daily life or get a "surface" diagnosis of your mental state. Our app is not intended to replace medical professionals, but serves as an entry point to quick help for anyone.

The Design

How was Happier designed? What did we have to face in the graphic design of the application? What did we research? At first, we considered some points for the interface.

Air out the interface

Do you feel better in a small room with black walls or white walls? The use of light colors (even white color in abundance) is mandatory.

Feather Icons

Simple and pleasing to the eye rounded icons, we used the free icon pack from Feather, which matched the criteria we chose for our icons.

Lightweight animations

Some animations, parallaxes, moving content, can be distressing and cause anxiety to some users. We use them only when necessary.

Immediate assistance

User support is important, especially in the context of our problem. We have therefore added free help numbers (e.g. in case of a user crisis). Also, a callback service within 24 hours can be very effective for a person in distress.

No time indications

Sometimes, signs of "timing" in the application can trigger anxiety in users (especially the time in the status bar). It is important to make them feel that they have all the time they need to perform the actions.

Less is more

Does the user really need that button? That information on their screen? How can we simplify his understanding? You have to be careful not to drown the user with content, he could get confused, not understand everything and end up leaving the application.

The Design Process

Here is one of the old wireframes designed for Happier. At that time, the application was full of buttons and statistics not necessarily useful to the user. We also determined that it was not necessary for the user to be required to register.

Logo Evolution

The first Happier logo was in beige and blue. After some design research and adaptations, it has evolved to more neutral colors and more pleasant to the eye for the user.

Design Evolution

The basic design was not at all suitable for the users we were aiming at. When we started developing, we had not done any research before. Since then, a brand identity has been developed.

Previous camera-based prototype

Strategy Change

After a certain time, we realized that the use of the camera would not be beneficial in the project, as it is difficult to extract reliable "emotional" data from it. So we removed this feature, which could bring us closer to a "social network" than an application completely focused on mental wellbeing. Finally, we focused on the chatbot by making it capable of giving psychological tests to the user.

How does it work for the user?

Discussion

Take part in a discussion with Hap, he will give you two psychological tests to determine your condition

Approach & objectives

Hap gives you activities to do. These can range from simple daily tasks to activities such as meditation or walking

Reporting

Two weeks later, you will have to redo the same previous tests. If there is progress, you can continue the activities that Hap gave you. Otherwise, you can be redirected to a psychologist or a hotline number to call

The Result

A small showcase of Happier's key features & screens.

  • Emotion Board

    Your Emotion Bard

    Your Emotion Board gives you an overview of your progress. You can find Hap's general indication of your day, as well as articles written by us to fit your situation.

    • General view of Hap's analysis
    • Recommended articles for the user
  • Hap Chatbot

    Meet Hap, your personal assistant

    Hap is designed to help you. It is able to give you the GAD-7 (anxiety) or PHQ-9 (depression) psychological tests. Based on your test scores, he will provide advice and activities to help you get out of this state. Every two weeks, Hap makes you retake these tests to evaluate your progress.

    • GAD-7 & PHQ-9 psychological score tests
    • Recommendations according to the user
  • Objectives List

    Small goals, big changes

    Here you will find a list of goals that Hap has given you. They are to be achieved within two weeks, if possible, before taking the first test. It is important to keep in mind that they are not mandatory, it does not matter if they are not done.

Technical Architecture
